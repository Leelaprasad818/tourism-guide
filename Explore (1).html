Taj Mahal, Agra Overview


Taj Mahal, located in Agra in the state of Uttar Pradesh, is widely considered as a symbol of eternal love. It is a monument built in the 17th Century. It was built during the Mughal rule, and can be summed up as a brilliant representation of grandeur that Mughal architecture stood for.

Built under the reign of Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor, Taj Mahal is actually a mausoleum of his beloved and consort Mumtaj Mahal. Its ethereal beauty typically signifies the beauty of his beloved, in whose memory this architectural genius was created. Taj Mahal is also one of the Seven Wonders of the world, popularly known for its white marble construction and surrounding greenscape. It also earns a place among UNESCO's World Heritage Sites.

More About Taj Mahal


The Taj Mahal has its name derived from the Persian language, and translates to the Crown of Palaces. Built by approximately 22,000 artisans, it is a true architectural wonder with its entry accessible through three gates, placed in the East, West, and South. The monument took approximately 17 years to build and is flocked by visitors all year round.


You can visit the monument in February to experience the Indian art and culture fest during the Taj Mahotsav that takes place here every year. It takes place between 18th and 27th February each year. Its majestic location at the banks of the River Yamuna only enhances its elegance and appeal for visitors, giving it a cool and calm vibe.


Architecture of Taj Mahal


Although a wonder from the Mughal era, the Taj Mahal in Agra carries a fluent expression of elements inspired from the Persian, Islamic and Indian styles of architecture. It is surrounded on its three sides by crenellated walls while its fourth side is adorned by the River Yamuna. Made of pristine white marbles from Rajasthan, the memorial also has over a thousand small gemstones covering its walls.


The monument is centred on an elevated plinth in square shape, with its four corners embodying the unique minarets. It has an arch shaped entryway, houses various crypts, and is typically recognised for its large dome in the middle. The actual tombs of Mumtaz Mahal and Shah Jahan are placed in a locked chamber under the main mausoleum.


Best Time to Visit Taj Mahal


The best time to visit the Taj Mahal is between the months of November and February. The time between these months is the most pleasant and travellers can enjoy visiting the lush gardens around the monument too. Other times of the year are mostly hot and damp, and can be uncomfortable for exploration.


How to Reach Taj Mahal


Located at a distance of 200 km from the national capital New Delhi, the Taj Mahal in Agra is easily accessible through roadways and railways. Buses and cars take the Yamuna Expressway to reach here. If arriving by train, book a ticket to the Agra Railway Station and hire a cab or taxi from there to reach the Taj Mahal.


Tips for Visiting Taj Mahal, Agra


Some useful tips to keep in mind when visiting the Taj Mahal in Agra include -

  • Take entry through the east gate to get the shortest queue.
  • Book a visit between November and February for exploration in the most pleasant weather.
  • Pick a free water bottle and shoe cover included with the ticket price.
  • Enjoy up to 30 minutes of free Wi-Fi.
  • Avail golf cart and battery buses from parking for easy exploration.
  • Use the cloak room to store luggage for free beside the ticket counter.
  • Pick an audio guidebook at just INR 118.
